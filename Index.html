About Mission Impartial:

Mission Impartial is a website that discusses the impact of oppression on the mental health of marginalized communities. It is also a platform containing a compilation of free and affordable mental health resources. The main objective of Mission Impartial, is to encourage conversations pertaining to how oppression affects mental health, as well as to provide adequate resources to aid further research and seeking affordable treatment for mental illness. The Effects of Oppression on the Mental Health of Oppressed People:

In the Caribbean, as well as many other places in the world mental illness and mental health has been stigmatised, inappropriately treated and ignored. This negative stigma or approach, has lead to the neglect of discussions about mental health and treatment for mental illness. In order to achieve social justice, it is necessary to evaluate the connections between mental illness, poverty and, being part of a marginalized/oppressed community etc. This platform contains information about these issues as well as free and affordable mental health care resources. Ref: Tackling social inequalities to reduce mental health problems

Oppression-Mental Illness

How does oppression lead to mental illness? The experiences and adversities of all marginalized groups are different. However, studies have shown the effects of oppression on these groups are quite similar. Facing discrimination, being bullied and being a victim of continuous hate crimes has the potential to lead to chronic anxiety, depression and other mental disorders. According to NBC News, two out of five LGBTQ youth have seriously considered suicide in the past year. Results from a survey done by the TheTrevorProject has determined that 46% of LGBTQ youth report they wanted psychological or emotional counselling from a mental health professional but were unable to receive it in the past 12 months. According to Medical News Today A 2015 meta-analysis found that BIPOC(Black, Indigenous People of Colour) who reported experiences of racism also reported that they experience the following mental health issues:
  • Depression
  • Anxiety
  • PTSD(Post-traumatic stress disorder)
  • Suicidal thoughts
